# MHM Method  
*The MHM Method: How People Actually Change.*

Hi, I’m Lukas Van der Spiegel *(author)*. I designed the **MHM Method** to understand how people actually change — mentally, behaviorally and structurally.  
This framework helps shape my way of thinking, decision‑making and long‑term growth.

These frameworks are free to use, adapt and share. I hope they offer clarity, structure and value in your own journey.

## Mental  
*The mindset layer — beliefs, clarity, identity, internal narratives.*

The Mental layer focuses on the **internal architecture** of change:  
how you think, what you believe, and how you interpret reality.

Use this layer to ask:
- What do I believe about this?
- What identity does this reinforce?
- What identity does this weaken?
- What assumptions am I making?
- What mental models apply here?
- What mental models limit me?
- What fears influence this?
- What desires influence this?
- What stories am I telling myself?
- What stories should I stop telling myself?
- What stories should I start telling myself?
- What is the simplest mental shift I can make?
- What is the deepest mental shift required?

## Habitual  
*The behavior layer — routines, discipline, consistency, repetition.*

The Habitual layer focuses on **what you repeatedly do**, not what you intend.  
Change becomes real when it becomes a habit.

Use this layer to ask:
- What habit reinforces this change?
- What habit destroys this change?
- What routine supports my identity?
- What routine weakens my identity?
- What is the smallest habit I can start?
- What is the habit I must stop?
- What behavior is automatic?
- What behavior should become automatic?
- What behavior drains energy?
- What behavior creates energy?
- What behavior compounds over time?
- What behavior has negative compounding?
- What is the daily version of this change?
- What is the weekly version of this change?

---

## Mechanical  
*The systems layer — tools, workflows, environment, structure.*

The Mechanical layer focuses on **systems**, not willpower.  
It is about designing an environment where the desired behavior becomes the default.

Use this layer to ask:
- What system supports this?
- What system blocks this?
- What tool makes this easier?
- What tool makes this harder?
- What workflow reinforces this?
- What workflow breaks this?
- What environment supports this?
- What environment sabotages this?
- What process can automate this?
- What process can simplify this?
- What process can remove friction?
- What process can remove temptation?
- What structural change has the highest ROI?
- What mechanical change makes the mental change inevitable?
