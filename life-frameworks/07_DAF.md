## What is the Decision Architectures Framework?

The Decision Architectures Framework (DAF) explains how to **design the environment around your decisions** so that good choices become easier, faster and more consistent.

Most people try to “think harder”.  
Decision architects **design smarter**.

DAF focuses on five pillars:

- **Trade‑Offs**  
- **Constraints**  
- **Risks**  
- **Reversibility**  
- **Opportunity Cost**  

Together, these pillars form a structure that removes noise, reduces uncertainty and improves decision quality.

---

## Trade-Offs  
*Every decision is a trade of one value for another.*

Trade‑offs force clarity.  
They reveal what you truly value.

Use this layer to ask:
- What am I gaining?  
- What am I losing?  
- What value am I prioritizing?  
- What value am I sacrificing?  
- What trade‑off would my future self choose?  
- What trade‑off would my identity choose?  
- What trade‑off has the highest long‑term ROI?  

Trade‑offs expose your **real priorities**, not your stated ones.

---

## Constraints  
*The boundaries that shape your options.*

Constraints are not limitations — they are **design parameters**.

Use this layer to ask:
- What constraints exist?  
- Which constraints are real?  
- Which constraints are imagined?  
- Which constraints protect me?  
- Which constraints limit me unnecessarily?  
- What constraint creates clarity?  
- What constraint creates friction?  

Constraints define the **shape** of the decision.

---

## Risks  
*The possible downsides — and their impact.*

Risk is not danger.  
Risk is **uncertainty with consequences**.

Use this layer to ask:
- What can go wrong?  
- How bad would it be?  
- How likely is it?  
- What is the worst‑case scenario?  
- What is the best‑case scenario?  
- What is the most realistic scenario?  
- What risk is acceptable?  
- What risk is unacceptable?  

Risk clarity prevents emotional decision‑making.

---

## Reversibility  
*How easy it is to undo the decision.*

Reversibility determines **speed**.  
Reversible decisions should be made fast.  
Irreversible decisions should be made carefully.

Use this layer to ask:
- Can I undo this?  
- How fast can I undo it?  
- What is the cost of reversing it?  
- What is the cost of not reversing it?  
- What version of this decision is reversible?  
- What version becomes irreversible?  

Reversibility is the **safety valve** of decision‑making.

---

## Opportunity Cost  
*What you give up by choosing one option over another.*

Opportunity cost is the **invisible price** of every decision.

Use this layer to ask:
- What am I not doing because of this?  
- What is the alternative use of my time?  
- What is the alternative use of my energy?  
- What is the alternative use of my attention?  
- What is the alternative use of my money?  
- What is the highest‑ROI alternative?  
- What is the lowest‑ROI alternative?  

Opportunity cost reveals the **true cost** of your choices.
