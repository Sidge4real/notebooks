## What is the Clarity Framework?

The Clarity Framework (CF) is a simple system for removing noise, choosing direction and acting with intention.  
Clarity is not something you “find” — it is something you **build** through identity, direction, priorities and action.

Use this framework when you feel:

- overwhelmed  
- directionless  
- stuck in loops  
- distracted  
- uncertain  
- overloaded with options  

Clarity is the foundation of effective decision‑making, consistent behavior and long‑term identity.

---

## Clarity of Identity  
*Who you are — and who you are becoming.*

Identity clarity removes internal noise.  
It aligns your decisions with your long‑term self.

Use this layer to ask:
- Who am I becoming?  
- What identity am I reinforcing?  
- What identity am I weakening?  
- What do I stand for?  
- What are my standards?  
- What is non‑negotiable for me?  
- What identity would make this decision obvious?  
- What identity would make this problem irrelevant?  

---

## Clarity of Direction  
*Where you are going — and why.*

Direction clarity removes strategic noise.  
It gives you a trajectory instead of random movement.

Use this layer to ask:
- What is my long‑term direction?  
- What is the next chapter of my life?  
- What is the next chapter of my career?  
- What is the next chapter of my identity?  
- What am I moving toward?  
- What am I moving away from?  
- What would “progress” look like here?  
- What would “misalignment” look like here?  

---

## Clarity of Priorities  
*What matters most — right now.*

Priority clarity removes operational noise.  
It ensures you invest your time, energy and attention where it matters.

Use this layer to ask:
- What is the single most important thing right now?  
- What has the highest ROI?  
- What has the lowest ROI?  
- What should I stop doing?  
- What should I start doing?  
- What should I continue doing?  
- What is urgent but not important?  
- What is important but not urgent?  
- What is noise?  
- What is signal?  

---

## Clarity of Action  
*What you do next — today.*

Action clarity removes execution noise.  
It turns direction into movement.

Use this layer to ask:
- What is the next action?  
- What is the smallest possible step?  
- What can I do in 5 minutes?  
- What can I do today?  
- What can I automate?  
- What can I delegate?  
- What can I eliminate?  
- What action reinforces my identity?  
- What action weakens my identity?  
- What action creates momentum?  

Action clarity is where clarity becomes reality.
